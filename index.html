The World

Before we dive into our meals let’s zoom out and ask:

How much does food impact our planet?

As it turns out, it’s a lot. From the water and land used to grow our crops and livestock and the gas released during production, the food sector leaves one of the biggest footprints on Earth.

Let’s see how it compares to other industries when it comes to water withdrawals, greenhouse gas emissions and land use

water

How much water does the food sector use compared to others?

Water scarcity is a global issue and the way food is produced plays a major role. Agriculture and food systems account for around 70% of global water withdrawals, making it the most water-intensive sector on the planet. Industrial processes and municipal (household) uses follow far behind.

Agriculture leads global water withdrawal

land

How much land is used to produce food?

Almost half of the planet’s habitable land is used for agriculture: almost 40% of global land is used for agriculture, including crops and pastures. Unlike industry or housing, food production needs vast open spaces, and this demand drives deforestation, habitat loss, and biodiversity decline.

How much CO₂ is released by the food sector?

While energy and transportation often steal the spotlight in climate discussions, agriculture and food systems contribute a significant share of global greenhouse gas emissions, particularly through livestock, and crop production. Globally, the agriculture sector emits over 20% of total emissions, second only to energy production.
